Understanding Topic Relationships
About Participant

The main participant that this topic is about. This is the subject of study.

Studying Participants

Participants who are learning about the main participant. They are the students.

Bosses

Participants who oversee and manage this topic. They are the supervisors.

Important: The "About Participant" cannot be selected as a studying participant or boss for the same topic. This prevents circular relationships.
Best Practices
  • Choose a clear and descriptive title
  • Provide detailed description for context
  • Assign relevant studying participants
  • Select appropriate bosses for oversight
Things to Avoid
  • Don't make the about participant a boss
  • Avoid vague or unclear titles
  • Don't leave description empty
  • Avoid inactive topics unless necessary
